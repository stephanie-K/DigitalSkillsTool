Do you want to opt out of the ‘open register’?

The electoral register lists the name and address of everyone who’s registered to vote.

There are 2 versions of the electoral register - the full version and the ‘open register’ (‘edited register’ in Northern Ireland).

You can opt out of the open register. This is the version of the register that’s available to anyone who wants to buy a copy.

Opting out will not affect your right to vote.

About the 2 versions of the electoral register

The full version

If you register to vote, your name and address will appear in the full version of the electoral register. The full version is only used for:

  • elections and electoral activities
  • preventing and detecting crime
  • checking applications for loans or credit
  • jury summoning in England, Wales and Northern Ireland

The open register

The open register can be bought by any person, company or organisation. For example, businesses and charities use it to confirm contact details.

If you do not want your name and address to appear on the open register, you can opt out.
